Acid Rain

Precipitation that contains nitric and sulfuric acids, is formed when nitrogen oxides and sulfur oxides produced from the burning of fossil fuels, enter the atmosphere.

Crop and Forest Damage

Air pollutants such as ozone can cause a great deal of damage to plant life such as reduced forest yields, reduced growth of tree seedlings, and an increased susceptibility to diseases, pests, and other stressors such as storms.

Ozone Depletion

Unfortunately, the ozone in the atmosphere is being greatly damaged by ozone-depleting chemicals such as chlorofluorocarbons, hydrofluorocarbons, and halons.
